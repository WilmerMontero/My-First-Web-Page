Introduction.

My name is Wilmer Montero, I work as an IT technician for the past six years, and this is my first attempt to learn something new.
I am a Garifuna as now called the Afro-Caribbean.

To be completely honest I don't like the word "Afro-Caribbean"

It's not like we carry an "afro" you know! The best way to refer to us is by Garifuna people as our language name.
The Garifuna people originated with the arrival of West African slaves who washed ashore in the Caribbean island of St Vincent.

Garifunas mainly live mostly on coastal towns and villages in the Central American countries of Belize, Guatemala, Honduras, and Nicaragua.
Some Garifunas live alongside the Creole population in main towns.

Garifuna communities live mainly on agriculture, fishing, and foreign remittances sent by relatives abroad.
Some are also involved in the technical trades. Garifuna who live in the rural areas mainly pursues a subsistence lifestyle,
while those in the urban areas live similarly to their Creole neighbors, pursuing professional occupations.
